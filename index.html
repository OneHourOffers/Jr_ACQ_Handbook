Step-by-step daily SOP
These are the exact steps you complete every single working day — in this order. No shortcuts. No skipped steps. This is how you become the highest-performing lead manager on the team.
01

Log in & pull up your tools

9:00 AM sharp · before team meeting
  • Open your CRM — do this FIRST before anything else
  • Open your dialer and confirm it's connected and working
  • Have your call script open in a second window or printed at your desk
  • Open your End-of-Day report template so it's ready to fill in as you go
  • Set your phone to Do Not Disturb — only calls you make count right now
02

Review your CRM — know your pipeline before you dial

9:30 AM – 10:15 AM · Hit List sweep
  • Work the Hit List in order: Tasks → New Leads → Missed Appointments → Follow Ups → Pond
  • Note how many qualified leads you've flagged for handoff so far this week — know your gap to goal
  • Do NOT touch Hot or Nurture leads — those are Sr. ACQ's
03

Dial — and do not stop dialing

1:00 PM – 3:45 PM
  • Start with your scheduled callbacks — sellers expecting your call are most likely to answer
  • Move to new leads after callbacks are complete
  • Target: 80 dials across your call blocks — track your count in real time
  • Do NOT stop dialing to do admin work — log notes and keep going
  • If a voicemail picks up: leave a short, confident message and log the attempt in CRM
  • If a number is disconnected: mark as 'Bad Number' in CRM and move on immediately
04

Run the 7-step call flow on every live conversation

Every live call · no exceptions
STEP 1OpenIntroduce yourself, reference the property, check if it's a good time.
STEP 2RapportConfirm ownership, ask how long they've had it (15 seconds max).
STEP 3ConditionAsk for 1–10 rating, dig for specifics on what needs work.
STEP 4MotivationAsk why they're thinking about selling — dig for the real reason.
STEP 5DecideReal motivation? → Handoff. No motivation? → Follow-up.
STEP 6HandoffLock in a specific day/time with the ACQ Manager. Send calendar invite.
STEP 7Follow-upGet a specific callback date. Log it. Set the task in CRM.
05

Log every call in CRM immediately after hanging up

Before the next dial · every time
  • Do NOT wait until the end of the day — log it right now, before the next dial
  • Required fields: Condition rating, motivation level, next step, follow-up date
  • Write notes as if a different person will call this seller tomorrow — give them everything they need
  • Update the lead status to reflect the outcome of this specific call (see Lead Status Guide)
  • If qualified: flag the ACQ Manager immediately — they handle the Hot promotion. See Qualified Leads section for the handoff process.
06

Qualify leads rigorously — only pass what's real

Quality over quantity
  • A qualified lead has BOTH: a clear condition problem AND a real motivation to sell
  • Do NOT pass a lead just to hit your number — bad leads waste the ACQ Manager's time
  • A qualified lead can answer: What shape is it in? Why do they need to sell? What's their timeline?
  • When passing a lead: call the ACQ Manager directly — don't just send a CRM tag
  • Brief the ACQ Manager verbally: condition, motivation, timeline, seller name and best number
  • Confirm the appointment is locked in with a calendar invite sent and accepted by the seller
07

Manage your follow-up pipeline daily

Never leave a lead without a next action
  • Every lead that is NOT qualified today needs a follow-up date set before you close the call
  • 30-day follow-up: seller has no specific timeline or mild interest
  • 14-day follow-up: seller has some motivation but isn't ready yet
  • Same-week follow-up: seller is almost there — one more conversation may close it
  • Same-day follow-up: if they asked you to call back this afternoon — put it in your calendar NOW
  • Never leave a lead in CRM without a next action — a lead with no next step is a dead lead

InvestorFuse CRM
Marking as Dead
When a lead is done, mark it Dead the right way. The CRM uses one action with one required dropdown — pick the correct dead reason every time so the pipeline stays clean.
How to mark Dead in InvestorFuse: On the lead, open "Select the next action" → choose "Mark as dead". A second dropdown appears: "Select dead reason." Pick the correct reason from the options below. Always leave a short note on the lead describing exactly what happened on the call.
The big distinction — qualified vs. not qualified. The first thing to ask yourself before picking a Dead reason: was this lead ever qualified? If the lead never qualified (we never really had a shot), use one of the top-level reasons: Not Interested · Do Not Call · Wrong Number · Invalid. If the lead WAS qualified — they fit our buy box, we had real motivation, we'd been working it — but the deal slipped away, use Opportunity Lost with the right sub-reason. Same word ("Not Interested") can appear in both places — what differs is whether the lead was already qualified before it died.
Not qualified — never had a real shot
🚫
Not interested
Jr. ACQ
When to use it

The seller still owns property, the number works, and they're not a DNC — but the opportunity is over. One of the four situations below applies.

Sub-reasons (write the specific one in your note)
  • Not selling anymore — seller said circumstances changed, they're keeping the property / off the market.
  • Blocked — you've tried calling multiple times and it never rings. Strong signal they blocked the number.
  • Fuck off — seller was hostile, cursed you out, told you off. Do not push further.
  • Too high on price — seller's number is so far above market that there's no deal path, even after a re-engage. Not a "we're apart" situation — a "no deal ever" situation.
⛔
Do not call
Jr. ACQ
When to use it

Legal-risk territory. Use this when:

  • The seller explicitly says "Do Not Call Me" (any version of it — "stop calling," "take me off your list," "don't call again").
  • The caller sounds like a litigator / lawyer — threatening language, invoking statutes, asking for your company registration, etc.
Rules after marking DNC
  • Stop dialing that number immediately — no follow-up calls, no sequences, no texts.
  • Write the exact words the seller used in the note, plus the date and time.
  • If they invoked legal language, flag your ACQ Manager in Slack right away so they're aware.
📞
Wrong number
Jr. ACQ
When to use it

The number is disconnected / out of service. The telco message plays, or it's an unassigned line. There's no seller to talk to on this number.

Heads-up
  • Do NOT use Wrong Number when the phone rings and a different person answers — that's not disconnected, that's just the wrong person. In that case, apologize, hang up, and check with your ACQ Manager on how to route.

Opportunity Lost
Jr. ACQ
When to use it

The lead doesn't fit our buy box and never will. Use this when one of the four sub-reasons below applies. Always write a short note describing the call.

What counts as a qualified lead
Your daily goal is 4 qualified leads. But not all leads are equal — and passing a bad lead is worse than passing none at all. It wastes the ACQ Manager's time and damages trust with sellers. Know the standard.
A qualified lead must have BOTH of these
1

Condition problem

The property has issues that create a discount. A perfect house cannot be bought at a wholesale price.

✓ Qualifies
  • Needs major repairs (roof, foundation, HVAC, plumbing)
  • Outdated throughout — old kitchen, bathrooms, flooring
  • Vacant or has problem tenants
  • Rated 5/10 or below by the seller
  • Fire, water, or structural damage
✗ Does not qualify
  • Move-in ready or recently renovated
  • Rated 8/10 or higher
  • "Just needs paint" — minimal cosmetic work only
2

Real motivation

The seller has a genuine reason to sell — not just curiosity. There must be a problem or pressure driving the decision.

✓ Qualifies
  • Inherited property they don't want
  • Behind on mortgage, taxes, or facing foreclosure
  • Divorce, death, or major life event
  • Tired landlord with bad tenants
  • Relocating or job change — needs to sell fast
  • Can't afford repairs and just wants out
✗ Does not qualify
  • "Just curious what it's worth"
  • No urgency, no timeline, no problem
  • Wants full retail price

CRM notes standard
Your notes are not for you — they are for the Sr. ACQ who will walk into the appointment using only what you wrote. Your notes are the handoff. If they're weak, the appointment dies before it starts.
🔒

The 8 must-haves — required before flagging a lead for handoff

Before you flag a Follow Up lead as qualified for Sr. ACQ handoff, all 8 of these must be filled in with real answers. Not one can be blank. If you can't get the answer, the lead isn't ready — stay in Follow Up and dig on the next call. Promoting the lead to Hot is done by Sr. ACQ / ACQ Manager — not by you.
1
Occupancy

Vacant / Seller-occupied / Tenant-occupied. If tenant: lease end date, are they paying, section 8 or private.

2
Beds / Baths / SqFt / Year Built

Confirm with seller, note any discrepancies between public data and what they tell you. "Public records say 3/2, seller says 4/2 — added room in 2012."

3
Overall condition — in their words

One sentence in the seller's own language. "Gutted, framing only," "lived-in but dated," "move-in ready," "grandma's house — hasn't been touched since '95."

4
The 5 major systems — age and condition each

Roof / HVAC / Electrical / Plumbing / Foundation. "Unknown" is only acceptable if you asked and the seller truly doesn't know — not because you forgot to ask.

5
Asking price

What number did they say. "Make me an offer" counts as a real answer but must be noted as such — don't write "TBD."

6
Timeline

How soon do they want to close. "ASAP" / "30 days" / "before end of summer" / "no rush." Must be a real answer — not a vague "sometime" — before flagging for handoff.

7
Why selling — specific motivation

In their words, not yours. "Inherited from mom 6 months ago, out of state, tired of paying $280/mo taxes" — not "wants to sell."

8
Decision-makers

Who has to be on the appt call. Just them? Spouse? Sibling co-heir? Anyone else on title? This kills more deals than price ever does.

The rule: If you'd be embarrassed for the Sr. ACQ to read your notes cold and walk into the appointment — the lead is not ready to promote. Get the missing info or schedule a second call.
Nice-to-have (collect if the conversation allows — not blocking)
  • ARV estimate — Sr. ACQ runs comps, you don't need to
  • Repair quotes if the seller volunteers them ("contractor said $65k")
  • Due-diligence response (DD)
  • Best contact time / preferred contact method
  • Mortgage status — paid off? current balance?
  • Other properties owned

Module 02 — Your Two Jobs

Condition & Motivation

These are the only two things that matter on every call. Master these and you master the role.

8 min read
Job #1 — Condition: What shape is the house in?
Ask for a 1–10 rating. Then dig for specifics. A number means nothing without context. "A 5" could mean a leaky roof, bad tenants, or foundation cracks. Find out exactly what's wrong.
How to ask about condition
YouUse this
"Can you tell me about the condition of the property — kitchen, bathrooms, roof, etc.? On a scale of 1 to 10, how would you rate it? 1 being a teardown, 10 being perfect."
You (dig deeper)If they give a short answer
"Any major repairs needed? How's the roof or the big stuff?"
You (dig even deeper)Secret weapon
"If I handed you a Home Depot card with an unlimited balance — what would you fix first?"
This gets them talking. Whatever they list is exactly what the property needs. Pay attention.
What you're listening for in condition
✅ Keep digging — good signs
Needs a new roof · Old kitchen or bathrooms · Foundation issues · Plumbing or electrical problems · Vacant or abandoned · Fire or water damage · Tenant damage · Code violations
❌ Harder to work with
Move-in ready · Just remodeled · Perfect condition · Seller rates it 9 or 10 · Nothing needs work
Remember: A perfect house is hard to buy at a discount. We look for problems — that's where we help AND make deals work.
Job #2 — Motivation: Why do they want to sell?
This is the most important part of the call. No motivation = no deal. Period. A house with problems but no motivated seller is a dead end. A motivated seller with a rough house = opportunity.
How to ask about motivation
YouOpening question
"What's got you thinking about selling?"
YouIf they gave a real answer — dig deeper
"Gotcha — what's been the biggest issue with it? Help me understand the situation a little more."
YouIf they said "just curious"
"What has you curious about it, if you don't mind me asking?"
YouStill vague? One more dig
"What would the sale of this property actually do for you?"
This question cuts through everything. If they can't answer it, there's no motivation.
What you're listening for in motivation
✅ Strong motivation — dig in
Behind on mortgage · Divorce · Inherited property they don't want · Bad tenants · Moving or job relocation · Behind on taxes · Can't afford repairs · Facing foreclosure · Health issues · Just wants it gone
❌ Weak motivation — follow up later
Just curious about value · Wants to see what they can get · No urgency · House is great · Not ready anytime soon · Won't share anything specific · Testing the waters
The golden rule
Pain = Deal. No Pain = No Deal. You are looking for people with a real problem. When someone has a real problem, we have a real solution. Your entire job is to find the problem.

The 7-Step Call Flow

Every call follows this exact sequence. Memorize it. Don't skip steps. Don't improvise the order.

The sequence — burn this into your brain
1

2
RAPPORT
Confirm ownership. Ask how long they've had it. Keep this under 15 seconds. Do not get stuck here.
3

Ask for the 1–10 rating. Then dig. Get specifics on what's wrong.
4

One or two light questions. Then move to condition. You're not here to make friends — you're here to find a problem.

YouThe ask

YouStill nothing — final dig

Do they have a REAL reason to sell? If YES → go to Step 6 (Handoff). If NO → go to Step 7 (Follow-Up). That's the only decision you make.

  • ✓

  • ✓

  • ✓

The seller situation — what you want to hear
  • !

  • !

  • !

  • !

What they say — these are green flags
Listen for these exact phrases:

"I just want to be done with it."  ·  "I can't afford to fix it up."  ·  "It's been sitting there for months and I'm paying taxes."  ·  "My tenants trashed it."  ·  "I need to sell before [deadline]."  ·  "It's too much to deal with from far away."
How to spot them fast
They volunteer information. They sound stressed or relieved to be talking to you. They know exactly what's wrong and why they need to sell. Contrast that with someone vague, laid-back, "just curious" — that is NOT your ideal seller. Move on.

Module 07 — When They Push Back

Objection Handling

Objections are not rejections. They're questions in disguise. Handle them calmly and keep moving.

6 min read
The 6 objections you will hear every week
Objection 01 "I'm not interested."
"Totally understand. I'm not here to pressure you at all. What's making you lean that way — is it the timing or something else?"
Why it works: You're not fighting the objection. You're asking a question that opens the conversation back up. Never just hang up.
Objection 02 "I already have an agent."
"That's great! We're different — we buy directly, so no listings or showings. Would it be okay if I asked a couple questions just so you have all your options?"
Why it works: You're not competing with the agent. You're positioning yourself as a completely different option — faster, simpler, no hassle.
Objection 03 "What's your offer?"
"That's exactly what my partner handles — he's the numbers guy. I just want to make sure I understand the property first so he can give you the most accurate number possible. Can I ask a couple quick questions?"
Why it works: You never get sucked into talking price. You redirect to qualifying — which is your job.
Objection 04 "I need full price."
"Makes sense. Can I ask about the condition? Depending on what it needs, there are different ways we can structure things that might actually work for both of us."
Why it works: You're not saying no. You're opening a door. Condition is what determines price — and that's your conversation to have.
Objection 05 "I need to talk to my spouse."
"Of course — that makes total sense. Would it make sense for both of you to be on the call when my partner reaches out? That way you both hear everything at the same time."
Why it works: You're not fighting the objection — you're using it as an opportunity to get both decision-makers in the room at once.
Objection 06 "How did you get my number?"
"Great question — we use public property records to reach out to property owners in the area. I don't want to waste your time — is selling something you'd even consider right now?"
Why it works: You're being transparent and immediately redirecting to the qualifying question. Confidence and honesty kill this objection fast.
The rule with every objection
Never hang up on an objection. Ask a question back. The seller who objects is still on the phone — that means the door is still open. The only dead lead is one who won't talk at all.
